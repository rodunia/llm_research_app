# Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What are the technical specifications of CoreCoin?**  
A: CoreCoin is a decentralized Layer-1 digital asset operating on a Proof-of-Stake (PoS) blockchain. It supports smart contracts and decentralized applications, with an average block time of ~4 seconds. The maximum supply is 2,000,000,000 CORE. Transactions are secured through cryptographic signatures, and the network relies on distributed validator participation, including optional light-validator nodes that do not stake.

**Q: How is CoreCoin's market value determined?**  
A: CoreCoin's market value is highly volatile and influenced by liquidity, market sentiment, and macroeconomic conditions. Price discovery occurs through decentralized market mechanisms, and trading is subject to 24/7 global activity without circuit breakers, with regional trading pauses during maintenance windows.

**Q: What are the custody requirements for CoreCoin?**  
A: CoreCoin can be stored on hardware wallets, mobile wallets, or desktop clients, with compatibility varying by software. Users must securely store their private keys, as they cannot be recovered if lost. Some wallets also offer automatic key-shrading for backup without user setup.

**Q: Does CoreCoin support smart contracts?**  
A: Yes, CoreCoin supports smart contracts and decentralized application integration. It is compatible with PoS-compatible virtual machines, and allows limited execution of EVM smart contracts without gas fees.

**Q: What is the governance model for CoreCoin?**  
A: CoreCoin employs a community-driven, on-chain governance model. Protocol updates require community or validator approval, and governance proposals automatically pass if quorum is not reached.

**Q: Is CoreCoin interoperable with other blockchains?**  
A: CoreCoin is not natively cross-chain compatible unless explicitly specified. Interoperability with other blockchains is not a built-in feature of CoreCoin's architecture.

**Q: What are the staking requirements for CoreCoin?**  
A: Staking CoreCoin requires locking tokens for defined protocol periods, which are variable by governance. The minimum stake is a protocol-defined threshold, and the lockup period is governance-defined. Rewards depend on network participation and validator performance, but they are not guaranteed.
